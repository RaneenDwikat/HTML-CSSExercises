When it comes to computer engineering versus computer science, people may wonder about the differences between the disciplines. While these terms are sometimes used interchangeably, they are separate fields that focus on different aspects of computer technology.1 Both computer science and computer engineering require an understanding of computer and information systems, as well as foundational skills such as coding and software testing. However, computer engineering jobs tend to focus on computer design and development, whereas careers in computer science can emphasize computing theory.
